Safety & Preparedness

Resources to keep you and your family safe

Home Fire Safety

Smoke Detectors

  • Install smoke detectors on every level of your home
  • Place them inside bedrooms and outside sleeping areas
  • Test detectors monthly
  • Replace batteries at least once a year
  • Replace smoke detectors every 10 years

Fire Extinguishers

  • Keep extinguishers in the kitchen, garage, and near fireplaces
  • Know how to use them (P.A.S.S. method)
  • Check pressure gauge regularly
  • Replace or service as needed
  • Never fight a fire if it's spreading rapidly

Kitchen Safety

  • Never leave cooking unattended
  • Keep flammable items away from the stove
  • Turn pot handles inward
  • Keep a lid nearby to smother grease fires
  • Never use water on a grease fire

Escape Planning

  • Create a home escape plan with two ways out
  • Practice your escape plan twice a year
  • Choose a meeting place outside
  • Once out, stay out - never go back inside
  • Call 911 from outside

Wildfire Preparedness

Wildfire season typically runs from [Season]. Stay informed about fire conditions in your area.

Defensible Space

Create and maintain defensible space around your home:

Zone 1 (0-5 feet from home)

  • Remove all dead plants, grass, and weeds
  • Remove dead or dying leaves, needles, and branches
  • Keep lawn mowed and well-watered
  • Trim branches that hang over the roof

Zone 2 (5-30 feet from home)

  • Create horizontal and vertical spacing between plants
  • Remove vegetation under trees
  • Cut grass down to 4 inches maximum

Zone 3 (30-100 feet from home)

  • Remove dead wood and debris
  • Thin trees and large shrubs
  • Keep grass and vegetation low

Evacuation Checklist

Your "Go Bag" Should Include:

  • ✓ Important documents (IDs, insurance, medical records)
  • ✓ Prescription medications
  • ✓ Water and non-perishable food
  • ✓ First aid kit
  • ✓ Flashlight and batteries
  • ✓ Cell phone charger
  • ✓ Cash and credit cards
  • ✓ Change of clothes
  • ✓ Pet supplies and carrier
  • ✓ Photos and irreplaceable items

Emergency Procedures

If There's a Fire in Your Home

  1. Get out fast - Don't stop for belongings
  2. Close doors behind you to slow the spread of fire
  3. Check doors before opening - if hot, use another way out
  4. Crawl low under smoke
  5. Once out, stay out - Never go back inside
  6. Call 911 from outside

Stop, Drop, and Roll

If your clothes catch fire:

  1. Stop where you are
  2. Drop to the ground
  3. Roll over and over to smother the flames
  4. Cool the burn with water and get medical help

Wildfire Evacuation

If ordered to evacuate:

  • Leave immediately - don't wait
  • Follow designated evacuation routes
  • Take your "Go Bag" and pets
  • Close all windows and doors
  • Turn off pilot lights and air conditioning
  • Leave lights on so firefighters can see your home
